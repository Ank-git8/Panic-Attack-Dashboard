Key Insights
Data-driven observations from the model
Summary
Highest Risk Profile
Patients with PTSD carry the highest avg panic score (5.74) and the lowest medication uptake (37.6%), suggesting a treatment gap in this cohort.
Trigger Severity Gap
Social Anxiety (5.77) and PTSD-triggered (5.77) attacks are 7% more severe than Caffeine-triggered attacks (5.38).
Dizziness is Most Common
51.7% of patients report dizziness — the most prevalent physical symptom — followed by shortness of breath (~49.5%) and sweating (~48.8%).
Gender Parity
Panic severity is nearly equal across genders (5.43–5.63). Male patients show slightly higher heart rates (+1.3 bpm avg) during episodes.
Sleep Deficit
The population averages only 6.48 hours of sleep — below the 7-hour clinical threshold — which may be both a symptom and a risk factor.
Treatment Coverage
Only 50.4% of patients are in therapy and 41.7% on medication. Combined coverage leaves ~25% with no active treatment.
